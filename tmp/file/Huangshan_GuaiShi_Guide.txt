# 黄山怪石图鉴 · Top 10 Famous Rocks of Huangshan

---

## 1. 猴子观海 (Monkey Watching the Sea)
- **Location**: Beihai Scenic Area
- **Description**: A rock resembling a monkey sitting on a peak, gazing across the sea of clouds.
- **Legend**: Said to be a Taoist monk turned to stone while meditating — forever watching the ever-changing clouds.

## 2. 飞来石 (Feilai Rock / 'Flying Rock')
- **Location**: Xihai Grand Canyon
- **Description**: A massive, precariously balanced granite boulder (12m high, ~360 tons), seemingly dropped from the sky.
- **Legend**: Thrown by the Monkey King during his rebellion in *Journey to the West*.

## 3. 梦笔生花 (Mengbi Shenghua / 'Dream Pen Blossoms')
- **Location**: Beginning of Cloud-Dispelling Pavilion trail
- **Description**: A slender rock pillar crowned with pine trees — like a writing brush sprouting flowers.
- **Legend**: Poet Li Bai dreamed of a flower blooming from his pen — symbolizing literary inspiration.

## 4. 仙人指路 (Xianren Zhilu / 'Immortal Pointing the Way')
- **Location**: Near Liangyue Pavilion
- **Description**: Two rocks — one tall & upright (the immortal), one low & extended (pointing finger).
- **Legend**: An immortal guided lost travelers toward enlightenment.

## 5. 金鸡叫天都 (Jinji Jiao Tiandu / 'Golden Cockerel Crowing at Tiandu Peak')
- **Location**: Near Tiandu Peak
- **Description**: At sunrise, a rock formation resembles a rooster crowing toward the sunlit summit.

## 6. 仙桃石 (Xiantao Shi / 'Immortal Peach Stone')
- **Description**: A smooth, oval rock shaped like a giant peach — symbol of immortality in Daoism.

## 7. 狮子抢球 (Shizi Qiang Qiu / 'Lions Competing for the Ball')
- **Description**: Two lion-shaped rocks facing each other, with a round rock between them — as if battling for a celestial orb.

## 8. 天狗望月 (Tiangou Wang Yue / 'Heavenly Dog Gazing at the Moon')
- **Description**: A dog-like rock silhouette aligned to face the full moon — echoing the lunar eclipse myth.

## 9. 武松打虎 (Wu Song Fights the Tiger')
- **Description**: A dynamic duo-rock scene: one upright (Wu Song), one crouched (tiger) — from *Water Margin*.

## 10. 苏武牧羊 (Su Wu Herding Sheep')
- **Description**: A lone figure-like rock beside smaller rounded stones — evoking Su Wu’s 19-year exile herding sheep in Siberia.
